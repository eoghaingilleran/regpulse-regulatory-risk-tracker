Firms should have effective systems and controls in place to detect and prevent market abuse.

Firms should monitor orders and transactions on an ongoing basis to identify suspicious activity and ensure that surveillance arrangements are proportionate to the nature, scale and complexity of the business.

Where a suspicion of market abuse arises, firms should submit suspicious transaction and order reports without delay and ensure appropriate escalation and governance arrangements.

Firms should maintain appropriate records of orders and transactions and be able to provide information to the competent authority upon request.
